The TSA Verification Pipeline

To ensure maximum transactional security, ООО "АВТОДОРТРЕЙД-БТК" utilizes a fortified Tank Storage Agreement (TSA) protocol. This ensures that every metric ton of product is physically verified in API-certified tanks prior to financial settlement.

PHASE 01

TSA & Allocation

Upon LOI verification, we issue a formal Tank Storage Receipt (TSR) providing technical allocation of product in vetted industrial tanks.

PHASE 02

Q&Q Verification

Buyer-appointed inspection agencies (SGS or Bureau Veritas) conduct full Quantity and Quality (Q&Q) analysis at the Port of Loading.

PHASE 03

Dip Test Validation

A Dip Test Authorization (DTA) is issued to buyer's engineers, granting physical access to tanks for molecular specification confirmation.

PHASE 04

Logistic Execution

Upon verified inspection, final settlement is processed (MT103/SBLC), triggering the Loading Act and vessel departure sequence.

Strategic Port Hubs

  • Rotterdam (NL): Deep-water terminal for trans-EU distribution. 420K CBM dedicated capacity.
  • Houston (USA): Strategic feedstock hub for Americas refining. Heavy crude and diesel blending.
  • Fujairah (UAE): Critical transit-point for APAC and MENA markets. VLSFO bunkering.
  • St. Petersburg (RU): Direct export hub for refinery primary output.

Quality Assurance

  • ASTM D1298: Precise Density and API Gravity validation ensuring contract compliance.
  • ASTM D93: High-fidelity Flash Point diagnostics for marine transport safety.
  • Sulfur Limits: Enforced Euro-5 and EN590 compliance audits (10ppm thresholds).
  • Molecular Scan: Gas Chromatography for hydrocarbon purity and water content testing.

Technical Storage Specifications

  • Storage Types: API 650 standard. Floating roof tanks for volatile light distillates; internally coated fixed-roof tanks for heavy crudes.
  • Injection Rates: Deep-water manifold systems capable of 12,000 to 15,000 metric tons per hour.
  • Security & Monitoring: Radar tank gauging (RTG) with continuous telemetry to our central logistics node.

Authorized Delivery Terms (Incoterms 2020)

  • FOB (Free On Board): Seller clears goods for export, buyer charters the vessel. Risk transfers at the ship's rail.
  • CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight): Seller manages transit and marine insurance to the destination port.
  • TTO (Tanker Take Over): Title of a loaded, floating vessel is transferred to the buyer while on active waters.
