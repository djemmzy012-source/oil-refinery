Crude Oil Fractional Distillation

Fractional distillation is the process by which components in a chemical mixture are separated into different parts (called fractions) according to their boiling points.

Crude oil is heated until it vaporizes. The vapor is then fed into a fractionating column. As the vapor rises, it cools. Different substances condense at different heights depending on their boiling points:

  • Refinery Gases: Used for heating and cooking.
  • Gasoline (Petrol): Fuel for cars.
  • Kerosene: Jet fuel and heating.
  • Diesel: Fuel for trucks and trains.
  • Bitumen: Used for road surfacing and roofing.
