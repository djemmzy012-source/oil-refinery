Gas Processing Workflow

From Wellhead to Export Terminal

A fully integrated value chain ensuring quality, safety, and commercial readiness at every stage—coordinated across extraction, processing, and multi-modal logistics corridors.

PHASE 1: EXTRACTION & SEPARATION
Wellhead Gas Conditioning

Raw gas undergoes initial separation of liquids, solids, and free water at the wellhead facility. Amine treating removes H₂S and CO₂ to meet pipeline specifications before entering the trunk transmission system.

PHASE 2: TRANSMISSION & COMPRESSION
Trunk Pipeline Transit

Conditioned gas enters the BTK high-pressure trunk pipeline network (72 bar operating pressure) with compressor stations every 120 km maintaining flow velocity and pressure integrity across 4,200 km of continental corridor.

PHASE 3: RAIL-FREIGHT CNG CORRIDORS
Regional Distribution Flexibility

For markets beyond pipeline reach, gas is compressed to 250 bar and loaded into ISO-certified CNG unit trains. Each 110-car train delivers 1.8 MMcf/day to industrial offtakers with just-in-time scheduling via BTK's rail logistics control center.

PHASE 4: LNG LIQUEFACTION
Marine Export Preparation

At Arctic-grade liquefaction terminals, gas undergoes deep purification, dehydration, and cryogenic cooling to -162°C. Four parallel liquefaction trains (5.2 MTPA each) ensure redundancy and operational continuity year-round.

PHASE 5: MARINE TERMINAL EXPORT
Global Dispatch

LNG is loaded onto ice-class carriers (174,000 m³ capacity) at dedicated berths with vapor recovery systems. Real-time cargo tracking integrates with BTK's marine logistics platform for end-to-end supply chain visibility.
